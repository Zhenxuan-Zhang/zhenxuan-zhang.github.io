About Me

I am a second-year Ph.D. student at Imperial College London, supervised by Prof. Guang Yang and Prof. Peter J. Lally, funded by the China Scholarship Council. My research develops agentic AI for medical imaging along three threads: motion-robust MRI reconstruction with implicit neural representations (IEEE TIP 2026), ultra-low-field to high-field MRI synthesis via self-supervised diffusion (IEEE TMI 2026, ISBI 2026 Oral), and verifiable radiology report generation with large language models (AAAI 2026, MedIA 2025). My prior M.S. work at Sun Yat-sen University focused on echocardiography analysis (IEEE TMI 2024, MedIA 2025). I build methods that are label-efficient, uncertainty-aware, and clinically deployable, with the long-term goal of translating foundation-model-driven AI into trustworthy tools for real-world radiology workflows.

Research Experience

Motion-Robust MRI Reconstruction via Implicit Neural Representations
Imperial College London · 2024 — present
Designed Progressive Refinement Implicit Neural Representation (PR-INR), which couples motion-aware diffusion for global structure recovery with implicit detail restoration for local fidelity, reconstructing 3D brain volumes from motion-corrupted 2D slices and outperforming state-of-the-art baselines across five public MRI datasets.
Outcome: IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, 2026.
Ultra-Low-Field to High-Field MRI Synthesis for Point-of-Care Imaging
Imperial College London · 2024 — present
Developed unpaired LF↔HF MRI translation methods for portable 0.05T scanners, including (i) a cyclic self-supervised diffusion model, (ii) a pretext-task adversarial learning framework, and (iii) reliability-guided selective generation that abstains on unreliable regions to reduce hallucination risk.
Outcome: IEEE TMI 2026; two ISBI 2026 Oral presentations; MICCAI submission under review.
Verifiable Radiology Report Generation with Large Language Models
Imperial College London · 2024 — present
Built agentic LLM pipelines for interpretable report generation with practicing radiologists: GEMA-Score, a multi-agent framework that decomposes report quality into clinically meaningful sub-criteria, and "Reason Like a Radiologist," a chain-of-thought and reinforcement-learning pipeline producing checkable reasoning traces.
Outcome: AAAI 2026; Medical Image Analysis 2025 (co-first author).
Multi-Dimensional Echocardiography Analysis
Sun Yat-sen University · 2021 — 2024
Proposed a cross-space consistency framework embedding pixel-, image-, and sequence-level tasks into a unified latent space for cardiac function assessment (validated on 10,908 patients across five datasets), and a text-guided bidirectional reciprocal cycle framework for label-efficient cardiac segmentation.
Outcome: IEEE TMI 2024; Medical Image Analysis 2025.
